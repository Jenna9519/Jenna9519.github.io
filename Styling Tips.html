The In's and Out's of Creating Your Own Style at Home

As much as there’s an art to making things look pretty, there’s a science to it, too. Equipped with the right rules and a healthy dose of creativity, you can make your home as beautiful as one found in the pages of Magazines. Besides, you know what they say — you can’t break the rules until you know them! Read on for styling tips and tricks for some of our favourite Interior Styles!

DO's DONT's
Accent with Bright Colours Too much Colour can be Overwhelming!
Mix Modern and Traditional Pieces Too many Traditional pieces dont create a contrast!
Dark Feature walls in small spaces grab attention!. Dark walls in Big spaces create a heavy atmosphere.

Contemporary Interior

"It Doesnt Matter if your taste is Modern or Traditional, Class or Retro. If a house has a good feel to it, it will always be a home." - Cameron Kimber, interior designer

  1. Simple and Uncluttered - a neutral, light pallet will create a clean open space.
  2. Open Spaces - As this may not be possible, try adding various colours and textures and using airy, light fabrics for window coverings.
  3. Colour - Adding a splash of colour to a neutral pallet can add a huge amount of character to a room!
  4. Rugs - Shaggy Rugs are back in style! Look for a netural rug with a bold geometric pattern.
  5. Wallpaper - Create a statement by adding a large-scale geometric to one of your walls!

Scandinavian Interior

"The Details are not the details, they make the design" - Charles Eames"

  1. Palette - Soft muted colours to create an elegant and sophisticated look.
  2. Furniture - Sleek, modern furniture style with light colours and material are a must!
  3. Decor - Sporadic but strategic placement of accessories such as; simple photo frames, clear glass baubles and portable lamps.
  4. Plants - It is important to have living elements of colour added to your space.
  5. Stoarge - Lots of cabinets and shelving to avoid unnecessary clutter. Less is more!

Bohemian Interior

"A Room should never allow the eye to settle in one place. It should smile at you and create fantasy." - Juan Montoya

  1. Colour - A mixture of colours and patterns are used - there are no rules!
  2. Furniture - Eclectic pieces from different time periods are recoevered and altered with bright colours and different textures.
  3. Ligthing - Colourful lanterns and Mooccan chandeliers are used. Natural light is also important but each space must be unique.
  4. Pattern - Many different patterns are mixed together; mostly bold tribal designs.
  5. Rugs - Layering Everywhere! Strong colours and geometric patterns will complete your Boho Interior!
